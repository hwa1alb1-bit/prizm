# Product

## Register

product

## Users

PRIZM is for accountants, bookkeepers, and small-firm owners who need a fast bank statement converter more than a broad finance platform. They convert client PDFs into spreadsheet output, work under deadlines, and need the workflow to stay simple even when the files are sensitive.

Daily users upload statements, review extracted rows, correct exceptions, export results, and answer client questions. Buyer users care about accuracy, privacy, retention, and whether the product removes manual cleanup without creating a new operational burden.

## Product Purpose

PRIZM is a lean bank statement converter. Its job is to turn PDF bank statements into clean XLSX and CSV output with as little friction as possible.

Trust remains a product proof point, not the headline. When PRIZM says files auto-delete in 24 hours, audit events are written, workspace isolation exists, or errors are traceable, the interface should show evidence through status, receipts, timestamps, audit history, clear labels, and documented controls.

Success means a firm can upload a PDF, understand the quote, review the extracted statement, export a usable spreadsheet, and move on.

## Brand Personality

Lean. Accurate. Calm.

PRIZM should feel like a premium utility for financial operations, closer to a focused converter than an enterprise compliance portal. The voice is plainspoken and exact. It avoids hype, legal fog, and decorative confidence. It should make conversion feel fast while making trust evidence available when users need it.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS gloss: vague trust copy, hero metrics, identical card grids, decorative security badges, gradient theater, and claims that sound confident without showing evidence.

Avoid policy-only compliance UX. PRIZM should not bury trust in documents while the converter itself stays opaque.

Avoid consumer fintech flash: neon dashboards, gamified money visuals, crypto-adjacent styling, and animation that makes sensitive financial work feel unserious.

Avoid slow enterprise compliance portals. Controls should be visible, but they should not make conversion feel heavy.

## Design Principles

1. Converter first.
   The default story is PDF bank statements in and clean spreadsheet output out. Trust and operations support that story.

2. Keep accounting in flow.
   Upload, review, exception handling, export, billing, and API-key work should stay calm and direct. The product should reduce cognitive load during repetitive firm operations.

3. Evidence over assertion.
   Every trust claim needs a surface-level proof point. Use status, receipts, timestamps, retention windows, audit events, connector health, trace IDs, and documented controls.

4. Name every state clearly.
   Users should never wonder whether a document is uploading, processing, waiting on OCR, ready for review, exported, expiring, deleted, failed, or blocked by a billing or permission issue.

5. Respect workspace boundaries.
   Multi-tenant context, roles, ownership, and audit history should be clear enough to prevent mistakes without adding noise to single-workspace Phase 1 flows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG 2.2 AA as the baseline. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus, reduced motion, color-blind-safe status states, readable density, and clear error recovery.

Do not communicate important states by color alone. Upload, OCR, deletion, export, billing, and permission states need text labels or icons with accessible names.

Write for professionals under time pressure. Prefer direct language, short sentences, and concrete remediation over vague reassurance.
